\begin{abstract}
The goal of this project is to implement constrain satisfaction model
for general case of \href{http://mathworld.wolfram.com/SocialGolferProblem.html}
{Social Golfer Problem}. The model is implemented in \href{https://sicstus.sics.se/}
{SICStus Prolog} using its \href{https://sicstus.sics.se/sicstus/docs/latest4/html/sicstus.html/lib_002dclpfd.html#lib_002dclpfd}
{CSP over Finite Domains library}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem Definition}
\quote{Twenty golfers wish to play in foursomes for 5 days. Is it possible for each golfer 
to play no more than once with any other golfer? The answer is yes, and the following table gives a solution.
Event organizers for bowling, golf, bridge, or tennis frequently tackle problems of this sort, unaware of 
the problem complexity. In general, it is an unsolved problem. A table of known results is maintained by Harvey.}

\mder{}{ $N \in \mathbb{N}$ ... number of players }
\mder{}{ $K \in \mathbb{N}$ ... size of groups}
\mder{}{ $D \in \mathbb{N}$ ... number of days}
\mder{}{ $G \in \mathbb{N}$ ... number of groups}

\mder{}{ Lets define groups as sets $$\forall i \in [G], j \in [D]: S_{i,j}$$
such  as 
\begin{itemize}
\item $$\forall p \in S_{i,j}: p \in [N]$$ Players are identified by numbers in given groups.
\item $$ |S_{i,j}| = K$$  Every group has exactly $K$ players in it.
\item $$\forall p \in S_{i,j}: p |{x | x \in S_{i,j} \land x = p }| = 1 $$ Every player is at most once in group.
\end{itemize}  }

We are looking for solutions such as every player is plaing exactly once in every day.
$$ \forall i \in [D]: \bigcup_{ j \in [G]} S_{i,j} = [N] $$
Every player plays no more then once with any other player. Which is same as every pair of players 
is in at most 1 group.
$$ \forall x,y \in [N]: |{S| \exists i \in [D] \land \exists j \in [G]: x \in S_{i,j} \land y \in S_{i,j}}| < 2 $$
